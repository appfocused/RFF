Pavel Ruminov

Director "I Will Be Around"

Pavel Ruminov was declared to be the “future of Russian cinema” by Afisha magazine. Originally based in Vladivostok, Ruminov starting his career directing music videos for various Russian artists and groups including Zemfira and Mumiy Troll. Upon relocating to Moscow, he redirected his efforts into making feature films spanning a diverse range of genres. He first came to prominence with the release of his third feature film Dead Daughters (2007), a homage to Japanese horror films which became the subject of a great deal of internet-based hype. He followed this up with the dark romantic comedy Circumstances (2009). His latest film, I’ll be Around (2011), won the Grand Prix at the 23rd Kinotavr Film Festival.
